    Frequently Asked Questions

    Explore the latest questions and answers

    • Who can donate the blood ?

      To donte,you must weigh atleast 100pounds and be atleast 16 -17 yrs old ,depending upon law. You must be in good healyh and able to pass the physical and a confidential heal,thn history assessment to help make sure that donation is safe for both you and recipient of the blood

    • Can I denote if I have a cold,flu or fever?

      No.To denote,you must be symptom free from cold,flu or fever on the day of denotion.

    • How can I prepare to donate blood?

      Before donating,eat a healthy meal and drink plenty of water. Go to your donation registration well rested and wearing a shirt with sleeves that can be rolled up above your elbows. Bring your donor card, driver's license or two other forms of ID.

    • Can I donate if I have recently had a vaccination?

      You may donate blood after most vaccinations if you are feeling well.you will have to wait to donate for 2-4 weeks after being vaccinated for chickenpox ,mumps etc..,

    • How long does donation takes?

      Whole blood donation takes about 45-60 minutes
      Double red cell donation takes about 30 minutes longer than a whole blood donation

    • What do I get in return for my blood donation?

      Blood is donated voluntarily, freely and without payment or reward of any kind. Blood must only be donated in the spirit of altruism for patients who need blood or blood products as part of their medical treatment. Blood is donated as an act of goodwill towards a fellow human being and nothing should be expected in return for giving this gift of life.
